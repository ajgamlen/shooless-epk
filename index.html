Shooless brings a sound and finesse that defies the status quo, from soaring rock to delicate jazz, embodying authenticity and sonic exploration.

What started with spontaneous jams in a West Auckland storage unit has blossomed into a dynamic musical force, inspired by artists such as Jeff Buckley, King Krule, Patti Smith, Mastadon, Jefferson Airplane, Rage Against the Machine, and PJ Harvey.

They frequent venues across the Tāmaki Makaurau live scene, captivating audiences with their energetic performances. And with highly anticipated shows across Aotearoa they're an act you don’t want to miss.

Their latest single 'Sunsets' is an absorbing preview of what's to come from their forthcoming album. Each track promises to unravel a narrative of personal struggle, self-reflection, and eventual resolution.

"Very few artists I have seen perform break the fourth wall between artist and audience in the way Shooless does."

- Hannah Nott, Ponsonby News

"Their essence and echoes of PJ Harvey, Jeff Buckley and Tool are boiled in the voodoo of King Krule."

"Guy Yarrall...belongs in the obsessive cannon of Tame Impala’s Kevin Parker, Kurt Vile and Beck ritualistically tinkering loop pedals and churning out channeled riffs."

"Different genre swords from jazz fusion and trip hop cut the ephemeral idea into perfected audio cathedrals."

- Floyd Lee, The 13th Floor
